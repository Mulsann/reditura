Understanding NHS Continuing Healthcare

CHC funding may cover 100% of your care fees - often called the NHS's "best kept secret"

Too many people entering the care system have no idea that their care fees could be paid in full by the NHS – yes, every penny! However, accessing this funding isn't straightforward and the NHS does not freely advertise its availability.

CHC is a package of care, commissioned and funded solely by the NHS, which meets all assessed health and social care needs, including accommodation costs. It's available to anyone with a "Primary Health Need".

You need a "Primary Health Need" - when the majority of care required is focused on managing or preventing health conditions. This is assessed through the Checklist, then formal MDT assessment using the Decision Support Tool.

Any adult aged 18+ with a Primary Health Need requiring care due to disability, accident or illness may be eligible. It's not about diagnosis - dementia or Parkinson's don't automatically qualify. It's about assessed care needs.

Absolutely not! Money and financial status should never be a consideration. Once found eligible for CHC, the NHS must fund the full cost of meeting all health and social care needs regardless of your wealth.

A Primary Health Need exists when the majority of care required focuses on managing or preventing health conditions (mental and physical). It's determined by assessing the nature, intensity, complexity and unpredictability of care needs.

CHC funding is paid by your local Integrated Care Board (ICB), which is the local arm of the NHS. Each ICB has a CHC budget to fund the care of eligible constituents in their area.
